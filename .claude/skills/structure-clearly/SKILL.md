# The pyramid principle
* Start with the answer first.
* Group and summarize your supporting arguments.
* Logically order your supporting ideas.

## Start with the answer first
* State the answer and then build the case.
* Follow a top-down structure, where each level starts with the main point and then with the details.

## Group and summarize your supporting arguments
* Form a logical pyramid, where the answer is at the top and arguments are logically broken down underneath in a group. Underneath each supporting idea you break down further into groups of more ideas or arguments until you form a pyramid.
* Prefer the magic number three for number of ideas in a group.

## Logically order your supporting ideas
* Ensure that the ideas in each group belong together, are on the same level of importance and follow a logical structure.
* There are three different ways to order ideas in a group:
	* In time: sequence of events that form a cause-effect relationship.
	* By structure: follows mutually exclusive, cumulatively exhaustive - break a thought into parts and cover all major supporting ideas.
	* By degree: present supporting ideas in rank of importance.
